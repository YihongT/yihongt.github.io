Refined Proposal: SafeCAFDR

SafeCAFDR means Safe Context-Aware Forecasting Deep Research. The project builds a forecasting agent that performs deep contextual research while proactively estimating and mitigating future safety and reliability risks.

Abstract draft

Context-aware agents can improve time-series forecasting by retrieving and reasoning over external information, but the same retrieval and tool-use capabilities introduce prompt-injection, leakage, hallucination, and unreliable-evidence risks. We propose SafeCAFDR, a forecasting deep-research agent that jointly predicts target outcomes and the future risk trajectory of its own reasoning process. SafeCAFDR uses a predictive guardrail to anticipate unsafe or unreliable states before they occur, then intervenes through context re-ranking, tool restriction, verification, or explanation revision. Experiments evaluate both forecasting accuracy and robustness under adversarial retrieved context.

Core contributions

  • Task formulation: Safe forecasting deep research, where a model must optimize accuracy and future safety.
  • Method: Context-aware forecasting agent plus predictive risk head and intervention policy.
  • Benchmarking: Forecasting tasks with adversarial and noisy external context.
  • Metrics: Joint reporting of forecast accuracy, evidence reliability, attack success, and intervention cost.

System components

RetrieverForecasterRisk predictorPolicy layerVerifier

  1. Retrieve context relevant to the target series and forecast horizon.
  2. Generate candidate forecasts and explanations.
  3. Predict whether the current reasoning path is likely to lead to unsafe, injected, or unreliable output.
  4. Intervene when risk is high by pruning context, requiring citations, reducing tool access, or asking for independent verification.
  5. Output calibrated forecasts with evidence and risk annotations.

Evaluation design

  • Forecast quality: MASE, sMAPE, CRPS, calibration, and ranking accuracy where appropriate.
  • Safety robustness: attack success rate, leakage rate, unsafe tool-use rate, and misleading-evidence reliance.
  • Agent reliability: citation faithfulness, evidence diversity, abstention quality, and cost.
  • Ablations: no retrieval, no risk predictor, no intervention policy, retrieval-only safety, and reactive-only guardrails.

Expected claim

SafeCAFDR should show that proactive safety prediction can preserve or improve forecasting accuracy under realistic context while substantially reducing unsafe behavior under adversarial context.
